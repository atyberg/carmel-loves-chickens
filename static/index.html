Carmel Loves Chickens

What do we do?

We are trying to change the current Zoning law of the Town of Carmel, NY

What is the law?

The current Zoning law of agricultural activities (chapter 156-17, article 3) states:

A. Farms, truck gardens, nurseries and other agricultural activities shall be permitted as principal uses, provided that: (1) The site size shall be at least five acres. (2) No building or structure used for any of the above purposes shall be located closer than 100 feet to any property line. Pens or buildings housing animals or runs shall be located a minimum 150 feet from any property lines.

What does this mean?

This law prohibits the majority of Carmel residents from owning chickens. To own and house chickens you have to be classified as a farm, your lot has to be at least 5 acres, and any henhouse or chicken coop has to be 150 feet, or 50 yards away from any property line.

Why should we have chickens?

  • We (residents of Carmel) should be able to to produce healthier eggs for ourselves and our families.
  • Chickens eat ticks, ants, spiders, and any other insect/bug they can find. That's good, isn't it?
  • This is for you people who already have chickens. If your neighbors complain to the town, they will force you to get rid of your chickens.

What can you do?

If you're from Carmel, talk to your local Town Supervisor, Zoning board or Building Department about being able to keep chickens on less than 5 acres and less than 150 feet away from a property line.

Frequently Asked Questions About Chickens

Q: Can chickens fly?

A: Chickens cannot fly, but they are able to flap enough to get themselves over some fences.

Q: Do chickens roam? Will they leave my property?

A: Chickens do roam, since they like to look for bugs and forage. They are omnivores, meaning they eat both bugs and plants. They obviously do not know boundaries such as property lines, so they will leave. Fortunately, you can do 3 things about that:

  1. They are smart and just as trainable as dogs, so you can train them not to go off your property.
  2. You can put a secure "chickenproof" fence up to keep them in.
  3. You can build a coop with a run (a run is an enclosed space outside the coop but connectd to it so the chickens can go outside while still being in the protection of their coop) and leave them in there most of the time, only letting them out when you are there to supervise.
